# Country Profile — United States (US)

**Language:** English (en)
**Currency:** USD ($)
**Regulatory body:** FDA (Food and Drug Administration)

---

## Buyer Psychology

Americans are the most responsive audience to bold, emotional, transformation-driven copy.
They believe in self-improvement, personal responsibility, and the idea that a solution exists for every problem.
They respond strongly to urgency, scarcity, and the fear of missing out (FOMO).
Skepticism exists but is overcome by social proof (numbers, testimonials) and guarantees.

**Core emotional drivers:**
- Fear of losing vitality, youth, or capability
- Desire for a quick, effective solution
- Pride in taking action to fix the problem
- Trust in American-made, clinically backed products

---

## Tone & Voice

- **Direct and confident.** No hedging. Every sentence makes a claim or moves toward action.
- **Urgent.** Time pressure is expected and accepted by this audience.
- **Aspirational.** Paint the transformation vividly — before and after.
- **Personal.** Use "you" constantly. Speak directly to the reader.
- Contractions are natural: "you're", "don't", "it's", "you've"

---

## Headline Formulas (US)

```
[Don't let AGE steal your BENEFIT]
[Finally, a way to OUTCOME without SACRIFICE]
[The #1 reason MEN/WOMEN OVER 40 struggle with PROBLEM]
[Why DOCTORS are now recommending MECHANISM]
[CLAIM — And it only takes SIMPLE ACTION]
```

**Examples:**
- "Don't Let Age Steal Your Sharp, Clear Vision"
- "Finally — Restore Your Energy Without Stimulants or Side Effects"
- "The Real Reason Your Eyes Keep Getting Worse After 50"

---

## Trust Triggers (US)

These phrases carry significant weight with American buyers:
- "Made in the USA"
- "FDA-registered facility"
- "GMP-certified"
- "Clinically studied ingredients"
- "Doctor-recommended"
- "Non-GMO / No artificial additives"
- "60-day money-back guarantee" (the gold standard)
- Large customer numbers: "50,000+ Americans", "Trusted by 127,000+"

---

## Discount & Pricing Psychology (US)

- Lead with the percentage off: **"65% OFF"** before mentioning the price
- Show original price crossed out: ~~$99~~ → $49
- Use "Today Only" / "Limited Time" — Americans accept this
- "Save $X" framing is very effective (concrete dollar amount)
- "Free shipping" is a strong conversion trigger
- Best value framing: "Most popular — 6-bottle kit"

---

## Urgency Language (US)

Strong urgency is fully accepted:
- "Today Only"
- "Stock is running low"
- "Don't wait — every day matters"
- "Limited supply at this price"
- "Claim your discount before it's gone"

---

## Forbidden Phrases (US)

❌ "Cures", "treats", "prevents", "heals" — FDA violation
❌ "Guaranteed to work" — implies medical guarantee
❌ "Clinically proven" without citing a source — FTC issue
❌ Overly British expressions ("brilliant", "bloke", "sorted")
❌ Overly formal academic language — feels cold to US buyers

---

## FAQ Tone (US)

Answers should be reassuring, confident, and brief.
Use "thousands of [Americans/men/women]" for social proof.
End answers with a positive forward-looking statement.

---

## Legal / Footer (US)

Disclaimer must reference **FDA**:
> "These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration.
> [Product] is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."

Distributor notice: "independent authorized distributor"
