# Country Profile — Denmark (DK)

**Language:** Danish (da) — or English for English-language campaigns
**Currency:** DKK (kr)
**Regulatory body:** Fødevarestyrelsen (Danish Veterinary and Food Administration) / EFSA

---

## Buyer Psychology

Danes share Scandinavian values of Hygge (coziness, wellbeing, quality of life)
and Janteloven (don't think you're better than anyone else — distrust of bragging).
They are highly skeptical of marketing, highly educated, and value authentic experiences.
Health and wellbeing fit naturally into Danish lifestyle — but it must feel genuine,
not like a sales pitch. Danes make thoughtful, well-researched purchases.

**Core emotional drivers:**
- Quality of life and long-term wellbeing (hygge framing for some niches)
- Natural and clean ("naturlig" resonates strongly)
- Honest, simple solutions
- Community and family health
- Skepticism of quick fixes — "does this actually work?"

---

## Tone & Voice

- **Warm but evidence-based.** More warmth than Sweden, less than IE.
- **Simple and clear.** Danes value clear communication over clever marketing.
- **No bragging.** Janteloven means you don't oversell. Let customers decide.
- Danish copy: informal "du" is standard

---

## Headline Formulas (DK)

```
[Hvorfor MÅLGRUPPE over 40 kæmper med PROBLEM — og hvad der virker]
[Den videnskabelige forklaring på PROBLEM]
[Naturlige ingredienser der støtter RESULTAT — her er hvordan]
```

---

## Trust Triggers (DK)

- "Naturlige ingredienser" (natural ingredients)
- "Klinisk undersøgte ingredienser"
- "GMP-certificeret produktion"
- "60 dages tilfredshedsgaranti"
- "Gratis fragt til Danmark"
- Real Danish customer names and cities (København, Aarhus, Odense, Aalborg)

---

## Discount & Pricing Psychology (DK)

- Always DKK (kr)
- Quality over price: "Kvalitet til en fornuftig pris"
- Long-term supply framing: "3-måneders kur"
- Free shipping: "Gratis fragt" — strong trigger
- No fake countdown timers or artificial scarcity
