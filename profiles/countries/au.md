# Country Profile — Australia (AU)

**Language:** English (en-AU) — British spellings, casual tone
**Currency:** AUD (AU$)
**Regulatory body:** TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration)

---

## Buyer Psychology

Australians are direct, plain-speaking, and deeply skeptical of anything that feels like
American-style marketing. They have a strong "tall poppy syndrome" culture — excessive
self-promotion backfires badly. They prefer brands that talk to them like equals, not marks.
They are health-conscious (active outdoor culture) and respond well to natural/clean positioning.
Authenticity is everything. A page that sounds real and honest converts far better than
one that sounds polished and pushy.

**Core emotional drivers:**
- Authentic, no-BS solutions to real problems
- Fairness and value (AU$ value for money)
- Natural and clean ("no nasties" is a real phrase Australians use)
- "Give it a fair go" — willing to try if the risk is removed (guarantee matters a lot)
- Sports/activity culture for male products: energy, performance, outdoors

---

## Tone & Voice

- **Casual and direct.** Almost conversational — like talking to a mate.
- **Anti-hype.** Understate rather than overstate. Let the product speak.
- **Honest.** Acknowledge limitations naturally — it builds trust.
- Australian humor (self-deprecating, dry) can work in moderation.
- Exclamation marks: use very sparingly. ALL CAPS for urgency: never.

---

## Australian Spellings & Expressions

**Spellings (British):**
- colour, favour, ageing, centre, recognise

**Australian expressions (use naturally):**
- "give it a go" / "worth a go"
- "no worries" (in CTA/guarantee context)
- "mate" (sparingly, only in very casual contexts)
- "fair dinkum" (genuine/real)
- "no-nonsense"
- "straightforward"

**Avoid:**
- American slang ("awesome sauce", "incredible deal")
- British stuffiness ("one might consider")
- Any phrase that sounds like a TV infomercial

## Headline Formulas (AU)

```
[The no-BS reason AUDIENCE over 40 PROBLEM]
[How MECHANISM is giving Australians their BENEFIT back]
[A straightforward approach to PROBLEM — no gimmicks]
[If you're AUDIENCE and PROBLEM — this is worth a look]
```

**Examples:**
- "The Real Reason Australians Over 50 Struggle With Their Eyesight — And What Works"
- "How a Natural Formula Is Giving Aussie Men Their Energy Back"
- "If You're Over 40 and Your Eyes Are Getting Worse — This Is Worth Reading"

---

## Trust Triggers (AU)

- "Natural ingredients" / "No artificial additives"
- "Manufactured in an FDA-registered, GMP-certified facility" (US quality respected)
- "Ships to Australia" / "Free AU shipping on X+ bottles"
- "60-day money-back guarantee — no questions, no hassle"
- Real Australian customer locations (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide)
- TGA-compliant language in footer/disclaimer

---

## Discount & Pricing Psychology (AU)

- Always use AU$ — this is non-negotiable
- "Save AU$X" is most effective
- "Free shipping to Australia" is a major trigger (AU shipping is notoriously expensive)
- "No subscription, no lock-in" — Australians hate hidden charges
- Plain value framing: "Best value — 6 bottles, one price"

---

## Urgency Language (AU)

Low-pressure urgency only:
- "Limited stock"
- "While stocks last"
- ❌ Avoid: Countdown timers, "LIMITED TIME — ACT NOW" — destroys credibility

---

## Legal / Footer (AU)

TGA disclaimer:
> "This product is a complementary medicine. Always read the label and
> follow the directions for use. If symptoms persist, consult your healthcare professional.
> These statements have not been evaluated by the Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA)."

Privacy: Australia Privacy Act 1988 — Privacy Policy must mention compliance.

Distributor notice: "independent authorised distributor" (British spelling)
