## PART 5 — COPY DIRECTION PER SECTION

### ALERT BAR
**Direction:** Lead with the strongest real offer (65% off) + the trust signal that overcomes the biggest objection (60-day guarantee). Three badges only: discount, guarantee, shipping.

**Avoid:** Fake countdown timers, "only X left" stock warnings (not verifiable), emojis.

### HERO HEADLINE
**Direction:** Use Loss Aversion formula: "Stop Overpaying for Electricity — [Starting This Month]". Lead with the pain (overpaying) and immediately promise relief (starting this month). The highlight should be the timeframe — immediate relief, not someday.

**Avoid:** Generic "Save Money" claims, too-good-to-be-true percentages in headline, exclamation points.

### HERO SUBTEXT
**Direction:** 
- Subtext 1: Acknowledge the real problem — "Your home is losing money to unstable electrical current every single day." Validates their bill pain.
- Subtext 2: Differentiate with mechanism — "StopWatt's patented EST technology stabilizes your current so appliances run efficiently." Gives them something specific to believe in.
- Must include: "No electrician. No installation. Just plug it in." — removes friction.

### BENEFITS SECTION
**Direction:**
- P1: Name the hidden cause — "Dirty electricity" and "reactive power" that meters still charge for. Use the phrase "Your utility company won't tell you this."
- P2: Emotional consequence — "They just pay the bill. Month after month. Year after year." Agitates the feeling of being trapped.
- P3: The bridge — Specific technology (EST) + real testimonial with month-by-month savings progression ($33 → $45 → $55). Shows it's real and improving over time.

**The "yes, exactly" moment:** "Most households never know this is happening. They just pay the bill." — Every frustrated homeowner has felt this.

### FEATURES
**Direction:** Lead with the 2 most credibility-building features:
1. **Stabilizes Electrical Current** — the core mechanism
2. **UL Certified & RoHS Compliant** — overcomes the "scam" objection

Then follow with practical benefits: appliance protection, zero installation, dirty electricity removal.

**Lead ingredient:** "Electricity Stabilizing Technology (E.S.T.)" — this is the proprietary mechanism that justifies the claim.

### SCIENCE
**Direction:** 3-step mechanism, extremely simple:
1. **Stabilizes** — EST technology smooths unstable current
2. **Filters** — removes dirty electricity and EMF
3. **Saves** — appliances run efficiently, bill drops

Language level: Accessible. No electrical engineering jargon. Each step answers "which means..." for the homeowner.

### REVIEWS
**Direction:** 4 reviews minimum, each mirroring a different customer profile:
- **Kevin H.** (Syracuse, NY) — Skeptic who was surprised
- **Melisa H.** (St. Louis, MO) — Data-driven, month-by-month savings

**Must-have outcome:** Specific dollar amount saved ("$35 first month", "$55 by month four"). This audience craves concrete proof, not vague "saved money" claims.

### FAQ
**Direction:** 8 questions minimum. Top 3 objections must be addressed:
- **Q1:** "Is StopWatt legit? I've seen energy saver scams before." → Answer must acknowledge skepticism, explain EST technology simply, cite UL certification.
- **Q2:** "Is it safe to plug into my home?" → Answer must emphasize UL certification, no wiring, no fire risk.
- **Q4:** "What if my bill doesn't go down?" → Answer must detail 60-day guarantee process step-by-step.

**Critical Q:** Q1 (scam objection) — if answered poorly, 80% of visitors bounce. Must acknowledge their skepticism before making any claim.

### CTA
**Direction:** Use "Get StopWatt — 65% Off Today" (specific offer) + "Risk-Free with 60-Day Guarantee" (removes final objection). Button should feel like the logical next step, not a hard sell.
