# MODEL C — Review Page
## Complete Strategy, Copy Framework & Implementation Guide

---

## 1. What Is Model C Review

Model C Review is an **evidence-first affiliate review page**. It mimics the structure and voice of an independent product evaluation — proof comes before the pitch. The visitor is treated as someone who already knows about the product and wants a trustworthy third-party assessment before buying.

**The core psychological contract:** "I'm not selling you anything. I'm showing you what I found."

This contrasts sharply with Model A (direct pitch) and Model B (upgrade comparison). Model C is the most editorial of the three.

---

## 2. When to Use Model C Review

Use Model C when ALL of the following are true:

- You HAVE affiliation with the product (you earn a commission)
- The product has strong visual proof assets (before/after, reference charts, ingredient images, lab photos)
- The product has verifiable certifications (FDA-registered facility, GMP-certified, Non-GMO, Made in USA)
- You can collect or model real testimonials with photo-realistic avatars
- The target keyword includes "review", "does it work", "is it legit", "[product] ingredients"

**Do NOT use Model C if:**
- You lack real imagery (the review page feels hollow without visuals)
- The product has no verifiable certifications
- You want aggressive scarcity/urgency tactics (review format undermines them)

---

## 3. The Review Page Psychology

Model C converts because it resolves **the trust deficit** — the visitor's biggest objection at the review keyword stage.

The visitor arriving on "[product] review" is asking:
1. "Is this real or a scam?"
2. "What are the actual ingredients and do they work?"
3. "What do real customers say?"
4. "What happens if it doesn't work for me?"

**The section order answers these questions in order:**

| Visitor question | Section that answers it |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| "Does this have any real science behind it?" | Scientific References |
| "Is it a legit company or sketchy operation?" | Why Choose (trust-c) |
| "What do actual customers say?" | Rating + Reviews |
| "What exactly IS this thing?" | Product Identity |
| "How does it actually work?" | How It Works |
| "What's inside it?" | Ingredients List |
| "What if it doesn't work for me?" | Guarantee |
| "What will I actually get from it?" | Benefits List |
| "I still have questions." | FAQ |
| "Okay I want to try it." | Final CTA |

**This order is non-negotiable.** Proof before product. Trust before education. Risk removal before benefit list.

## 5. Section-by-Section Copy Formulas

### ⚠️ The Review Voice
Every section is written from the perspective of an informed third party who has researched and evaluated the product. Never use "Buy now, you need this!" energy. Use "Here's what the evidence shows" energy.

Voice characteristics:
- Measured, not breathless
- Evidence-cited, not hype-driven
- Empathetic to the reader's skepticism
- Slightly journalistic ("we found", "clinical observations show", "independent testing confirms")

---

### 5.1 — PROMO BANNER

**Purpose:** Create a value context before the hero. Two facts, not three. One urgency signal + one risk-removal signal.

**Formula:**
```
Left badge:  [Urgency] — "Save $[X] — Today Only!" / "Over [X]% OFF This Week"
Right badge: [Risk reversal] — "[X]-Day Money-Back Guarantee" / "Free Shipping + Free Returns"
```

**Icons:** Clock for urgency. Shield for guarantee.

**What NOT to write:** No claims about the product itself. No health language. Pure offer framing.

---

### 5.2 — HERO C

**Purpose:** State the outcome the visitor wants. Show the product. Establish the discount context. Get them interested enough to read on.

**Headline formula:**
```
Don't [Wait / Ignore / Accept] [Negative Consequence]:
[Action Phrase] to [Achieve Desired Outcome]
```

Examples across niches:
- Eye health: "Don't Wait Until It's Too Late: Try This Morning Habit to Get Your Vision Back to 20/20"
- Weight: "Don't Let Another Year Pass: Start This Daily Habit to Finally Drop the Stubborn Weight"
- Joint: "Don't Accept Pain as Inevitable: This Simple Formula is Restoring Mobility for Thousands"
- Hair: "Don't Give Up on Your Hair: This Breakthrough Is Reversing Thinning for Men Over 40"
- Sleep: "Don't Keep Suffering Through Restless Nights: This Nightly Routine Transforms Your Sleep"

**Highlight rule:** The highlighted span should be the most emotionally resonant part — usually the outcome phrase.

**Three body paragraphs (strict formula):**

**Paragraph 1 — The Problem + Question Hook:**
```
Is your [symptom]? Do you [daily frustration]? Every [time unit] you wait,
[consequence] — but what if you could [reversal]?
```
Example: "Is your vision getting worse every year, even with stronger prescriptions? Do you miss the days when reading was effortless and driving at night didn't feel dangerous? Every day you wait, the blur can get worse — but what if you could not only stop it, but actually reverse it?"

**Paragraph 2 — The Solution Bridge:**
```
[Product name] helps [restore/improve/support] [outcome] by [mechanism description]
— not just [the symptom]. It's the [adjective] way to [benefit chain].
```
Example: "Reticlear helps restore sharp, comfortable vision by nourishing your eyes from within and addressing the real causes of vision decline — not just the symptoms. It's the natural way to protect your eyesight, reduce strain, and reclaim your visual independence."

**Paragraph 3 — The Vision / Dream Outcome:**
```
Imagine [vivid sensory positive outcome]. [Second vivid positive outcome].
[Aspirational statement — "That clarity isn't a distant memory — it can be your new reality."]
```
Example: "Imagine waking up with crisp focus, seeing your loved ones' faces in perfect detail, and reading without squinting or fatigue. That clarity isn't a distant memory — it can be your new reality."

**Pricing display:** Always show both prices. Strikethrough on original.
- Format: `[Current price]` bold + `[Original price]` strikethrough + muted

**Hero offer label:** One line above the price. Formula: "Try [Product] for [X]% OFF Today!"

---

### 5.3 — SCIENTIFIC REFERENCES

**Purpose:** Visual credibility anchor. Shows the product's formula is grounded in published research.

**Title:** Keep it simple — "Scientific References:" or "Clinical Studies Behind [Product]:"

**Image:** A collage of study citations, journal covers, or research reference screenshots provided by the manufacturer's affiliate materials. NEVER fake or hallucinate study citations.

**What this section does psychologically:** It signals "this is not a random supplement — there are real papers behind this." The visitor doesn't read the references. They SEE them. The visual alone does the job.

---

### 5.4 — TRUST-C (Why Choose)

**Purpose:** Legitimacy proof through certifications.

**Headline formula:** "Why Choose [Product Name]?" or "Why [Product] Stands Apart"

**Four badges (standard layout):**
1. Made in USA / Made in [Country] — Manufacturing location
2. FDA-Compliant Facility / FDA-Registered Facility — Regulatory compliance
3. GMP-Certified Production — Quality standard
4. 100% Natural & Safe / Non-GMO / Third-Party Tested — Purity signal

**Image badges:** Use the official certification badge images from the manufacturer. Each card: image (80px tall) + bold title + 1 sentence description.

**Card copy formula:**
```
Title: [Certification Label]
Subtitle: [What this means for the customer in 1 sentence]
```
Example: "GMP-Certified Production — Good Manufacturing Practice certification guaranteeing pharmaceutical-grade quality and purity standards."

**NEVER fabricate certifications.** If the product isn't GMP-certified, do not include that badge.

---

### 5.5 — RATING

**Purpose:** Social proof number. The aggregate rating acts as a "consensus signal" before individual testimonials.

**Headline:** "Our customers say" (neutral, not salesy)

**Display:** Numeric rating (4.8/5, 4.97/5) + 4 full stars + 1 half star (works visually for 4.5–5.0 range)

**Count line:** "based on [X,XXX]+ reviews!" — always with "based on" to feel factual, not boastful.

**Where to get these numbers:** Use the official product page stats. If not available, use conservative numbers. Do NOT invent. Minimum 500 reviews to use this block.

---

### 5.6 — REVIEWS (using existing reviews block)

**Purpose:** Individual social proof. Three real testimonials with photos.

**Review card formula:**
```
Photo: [circular avatar — ideally a real photo from manufacturer materials]
Rating: 5.0 ★★★★★
Label: Verified Purchase / Verified Customer
Quote: [Specific outcome achieved, not generic praise]
Name: [First name, Last initial.]
Location: [City, State/Country]
```

**Quote copy formula — CRITICAL:**
Each review must contain:
1. A "before" reference (age, old state, skepticism)
2. A specific outcome achieved
3. An emotional payoff (feeling, not just physical result)

**Examples across niches:**

Eye health: "At 58, I thought declining vision was just part of getting older. Reticlear proved me wrong. My eyes feel 20 years younger — sharp and comfortable!"

Weight: "I'd tried everything and given up. After 6 weeks on [Product], I'm down 17 lbs and my clothes actually fit again. I feel like myself for the first time in years."

Joint: "I couldn't play with my grandchildren without limping. Six weeks in, I'm back to morning walks. My doctor was genuinely surprised."

Sleep: "Years of staring at the ceiling at 2am. Within a week of [Product] I was sleeping 7 hours straight. My husband says I'm a different person."

Hair: "I was horrified by my hairline at 43. Three months in, my barber noticed the difference before I even mentioned it."

**NEVER invent names, photos, or testimonials.** Use manufacturer-provided testimonials or clearly modeled composites.

---

### 5.7 — PRODUCT IDENTITY ("What Is [Product]?")

**Purpose:** For the visitor who got convinced enough by the reviews to want to understand what they're actually buying.

**Headline formula:** "What Is [Product Name]?" — simple, direct, educational.

**Image:** The "what is it" hero visual from the manufacturer (typically shows the product with benefit callouts, or a clean lifestyle image). 

**Three paragraphs (strict formula):**

**Paragraph 1 — The Breakthrough + What It Does:**
```
[Product] is a [adjective] [product category] created to [primary benefit 1],
[primary benefit 2], and [primary benefit 3]. Rather than [dismissing the problem
as inevitable], [Product] empowers you to [active benefit framing] with a
[evidence-based descriptor] approach that [mechanism summary].
```

**Paragraph 2 — What's Inside + Manufacturing:**
```
Inside each [capsule/tablet/serving] is a proprietary [blend/formula/fusion]
of [ingredient types — e.g., "clinically-tested vitamins, powerful antioxidants,
and premium plant extracts"] chosen specifically for [specific function].
Made in [country] at an [certification]-certified production facility, [Product]
delivers [quality promise].
```

**Paragraph 3 — Who It's For (Broadening Eligibility):**
```
Whether your [symptom 1], your [body/health] [symptom 2], or you're worried
about [condition], [Product] gives you [hope + path forward].
```

---

### 5.8 — HOW IT WORKS

**Purpose:** Mechanistic education. Transforms the product from "a supplement" to "a targeted solution."

**Headline formula:** "How Does [Product] Work?" or "How [Product] Delivers Results"

**Intro formula:**
```
[Product] delivers results through [N] scientifically-validated mechanisms:
```
(Always 3 mechanisms for the reference page structure. Can be 2 for simpler products.)

**Image:** The "how it works" diagram from manufacturer materials.

**Step copy formula:**
```
Title: [Number]. [Mechanism Name & Primary Action]
Body: [What's happening biologically] + [Specific ingredients involved]
+ <strong>[What the customer feels/experiences as a result]</strong>
```

**The structure:** Start with the biological truth → name the ingredients involved → end with the experiential benefit in bold.

**Example (eye health):**
```
1. Macular Support & Regeneration
Sharp central vision requires optimal macular health. [Product] provides therapeutic doses of lutein and zeaxanthin — the only carotenoids found in the macula — along with vitamins A and C, to <strong>strengthen this vital tissue, enhance visual acuity, and support the cells responsible for detailed, clear sight</strong>.
```

**Niche mechanism templates:**

| Niche | Mechanism 1 | Mechanism 2 | Mechanism 3 |
|-------|------------|------------|------------|
| Eye | Macular Support | Free Radical Neutralization | Blue Light Filtration |
| Weight | Metabolic Activation | Appetite Regulation | Fat Oxidation Support |
| Joint | Inflammation Reduction | Cartilage Regeneration | Synovial Fluid Restoration |
| Sleep | Cortisol Regulation | Melatonin Pathway Support | Deep Sleep Cycle Extension |
| Hair | DHT Blocking | Follicle Blood Flow | Keratin Matrix Support |
| Blood Sugar | Glucose Transport Optimization | Insulin Sensitivity | Pancreatic Function Support |

---

### 5.9 — INGREDIENTS LIST

**Purpose:** Transparency proof. Informed buyers want to verify the formula before purchasing.

**Headline formula:** "[Product]'s Key Ingredients" or "The [N] Ingredients Inside [Product]"

**Intro formula:**
```
Powered by clinically-studied natural ingredients, [Product]'s proprietary formula provides
the exact [nutrients/compounds] your [body/eyes/metabolism] needs for [benefit 1], [benefit 2],
and [benefit 3].
```

**Image:** The ingredients panel image from manufacturer materials.

**Each ingredient item formula:**
```
[Number]. [Ingredient Name]:
[What it is biologically — 1 phrase] + [What it does mechanistically] +
[What the customer experiences/avoids as a result].
```

**CRITICAL anti-hallucination rule:** Only list ingredients found on the official product label or official product page. Never add ingredients not present. Never add dosages you haven't confirmed. If dosage is unknown, omit it.

**Ingredient copy template (universal):**
- Strong antioxidant: "A [potency] antioxidant that [neutralizes/combats] [specific damage type], protecting [tissue] from [consequence] and [secondary benefit]."
- Vitamin: "[Vitamin name] [optimizes/supports/enhances] [specific function], while also [secondary function]."
- Botanical: "[Plant name], widely studied for [known effect]. It [mechanism] and [benefit confirmation]."
- Amino acid: "A [fundamental/essential] [amino acid/compound] essential for [organ/system] function. [Primary mechanism] and [prevents/supports] [specific condition]."

---

### 5.10 — GUARANTEE

**Purpose:** Risk removal. Mid-page placement (not just footer) maximizes impact — it appears right after the "I need to try this" moment triggered by the ingredients section.

**Image:** The official guarantee badge (30-day, 60-day, or 90-day).

**Headline formula:** "Protected by Our [X]-Day [Money-Back] Guarantee"

**Body copy formula:**
```
Yes. We stand behind [Product] with a comprehensive [X]-day money-back guarantee.
If you're not [specific positive outcome — e.g., "thrilled with your sharper vision"],
simply reach out within [X] days for a full refund. We're so confident in our formula
that we make this promise to every single customer — <strong>your satisfaction is guaranteed</strong>.
```

**CTA text:** "Order now with zero risk." (never "Buy now" — "zero risk" is the key frame)

**CRITICAL:** Only state the guarantee period the manufacturer actually offers. Verify on the official product page. Do NOT use 60-day if the product only offers 30-day.

---

### 5.11 — BENEFITS LIST

**Purpose:** Confirmation stage — "So what exactly do I get?" This comes after the guarantee because the visitor's objections are now mostly resolved.

**Headline formula:** "[Product] Benefits" or "What [Product] Delivers"

**Intro formula:**
```
Unlock the remarkable benefits of [Product]'s advanced natural formula.
From [early/rapid benefit] to [long-term protection], your [transformation] starts here.
```

**Image:** A benefits comparison chart or summary graphic from manufacturer.

**Benefits list rules:**
- 8–12 items
- Each item = one specific, concrete outcome
- Mix immediate benefits (feels fast) with long-term protection (builds commitment)
- No vague claims ("improves health") — always specific ("Sharpens night vision for safer driving")
- End with a manufacturing/safety benefit to anchor trust

**Benefits formula template:**
```
[Action verb] [specific outcome] [without negative alternative or with positive qualifier].
```

Examples:
- "Restores natural [outcome] without [invasive alternative] or [pharmaceutical alternative]."
- "Protects against [specific condition] and [related condition]."
- "Eliminates [specific symptom] from [modern stressor — screen, stress, etc.]."
- "Sharpens [specific function] for [real-life application]."
- "Defends [tissue/structure] from [specific damage type]."
- "Relieves [chronic issue] for [duration/qualifier]."
- "Boosts [circulation/function] to support [healthy state]."
- "Ensures [long-term benefit] with [safety qualifier]."
- "Trusted [adjective] formula, completely [safety claim] for [duration of use]."
- "Crafted in [certification] facilities." (last item — closes with legitimacy)

---

### 5.12 — FAQ

Minimum 6 questions. Must answer in this order:
1. Safety / long-term use
2. Timeline / when will I see results
3. Does it actually work (the big skeptical question)
4. Shipping time
5. Is it a subscription (NO — always confirm one-time charge)
6. How to use it / dosage

**FAQ copy tone:** Confident but honest. Acknowledge uncertainty where it exists ("most users experience... individual results may vary").

---

### 5.13 — FINAL CTA

**Purpose:** The last conversion push. Full-width gradient section with the product image + price comparison + CTA.

**Headline formula:** "Order Your Discounted [Product] Bottle Now!" or "Claim Your [X]% OFF Before It's Gone"

**Image:** The multi-bottle offer image (e.g., 1, 3, and 6 bottle packages).

**Price display:**
```
Regular Price: ~~$[original]/per bottle~~
Only for: $[current]/per bottle  (large, bold)
```

**CTA text formula:** "GET YOUR [X]% DISCOUNT NOW" — always with percentage, always uppercase, always with cart icon.

---

### 5.14 — FLOATING CTA

A persistent order button that follows the visitor throughout the page. Appears bottom-right. Pulsing animation. Cart icon.

**Text:** "Order Now" — kept short for the constrained space.

---

## 6. The 7-Sweep Edit Adapted for Model C Review

Run each sweep on the complete JSON text fields before generation:

**Sweep 1 — Evidence check:** Every mechanism claim in "How It Works" cites a specific ingredient by name. Remove any claim without an ingredient.

**Sweep 2 — Voice consistency:** Scan all copy for first-person sales language ("You must try this!"). Replace with editorial framing ("clinical observations show", "users report", "the formula delivers").

**Sweep 3 — Specificity pass:** Every vague word ("better", "improved", "enhanced") must become specific. "Better vision" → "sharper central vision clarity at distances over 20 feet."

**Sweep 4 — Guarantee accuracy:** Verify the guarantee days match the official product page. If unknown, use 30-day as the conservative default.

**Sweep 5 — Certification audit:** Every badge in trust-c must be verifiable on the official product page. Remove any badge that isn't confirmed.

**Sweep 6 — Legal language check:** Confirm no "cures", "treats", "diagnoses", or "FDA approved" language anywhere.

**Sweep 7 — Image slot audit:** Confirm every JSON image field points to a real local file (not an external URL, not a placeholder). If images aren't ready, use placeholder URLs and flag them.

## 10. Anti-Hallucination Guardrails

**NEVER invent:**
- Ingredient names or dosages not on the official label
- Certification claims not verifiable on the official product page
- Study citations or study percentages
- Testimonial names, locations, or quotes
- Guarantee days beyond what the manufacturer offers
- Price discounts not currently active on the official page

**ALWAYS verify from the official product page:**
- Exact guarantee period (30/60/90-day)
- Exact certifications (FDA-registered vs. FDA-approved — these are DIFFERENT)
- Exact current price and original price
- Actual ingredients list (from the Supplement Facts panel)
- Real testimonials (use manufacturer-provided, model composites only if labeled)

**If a field cannot be verified:** Leave the JSON placeholder text and note `[VERIFY: field_name]` in a comment. Flag it for the owner before generating.
